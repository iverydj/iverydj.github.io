## Research Focus

My research centers on quantitative defect and interface analysis in next-generation solid-state materials, especially oxide semiconductors and two-dimensional (2D) systems. I connect material-level evidence to device-level behavior through integrated measurement, modeling, and interpretation workflows.

The research strategy combines:

1. Defect-resolved electrical and optical spectroscopy
2. Nanoscale spectroscopic mapping
3. Device fabrication and characterization
4. Explainable AI for physically interpretable experimental analysis

## 1. Defect and Optical Spectroscopy {.section-title}

Defects in oxide and 2D materials affect mobility, threshold behavior, stability, and optical response. The key objective is not only to detect defects, but to quantify their energy levels, densities, kinetics, and interfacial roles.

- **DLTS and PICTS** are used to extract deep-level parameters and correlate them with transistor metrics.
- **Raman and photoluminescence spectroscopy** track strain, doping, phase behavior, and defect-induced changes.
- **XPS and spectroscopic ellipsometry** link chemistry and optical constants to electrical performance trends.

## 2. Explainable AI for Experimental Data {.section-title}

Experimental datasets are often small, condition-dependent, and heterogeneous. In this environment, AI should improve interpretation and reproducibility, not only predictive accuracy.

- End-to-end pipelines are designed for preprocessing, feature extraction, model training, and robust validation.
- Explainability methods identify decisive spectral or electrical regions and support hypothesis generation.
- Data-limited strategies, including physics-aware preprocessing and stability checks, improve generalization.

## 3. Advanced Electronic and Photonic Devices {.section-title}

Material quality alone does not determine device outcomes. Performance and reliability depend on how defects and interfaces are managed in structure and process design.

- Device behavior is analyzed through quantitative current-voltage and transient-response interpretation.
- Oxide and 2D platforms are evaluated for logic-oriented and emerging architectures.
- FDTD simulation and inverse-design strategies are used when optical-field engineering is required.
